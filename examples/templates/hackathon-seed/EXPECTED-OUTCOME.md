## A good drive yields

- **A coherent ranked board off the seed.** `vend steer` reads the one-line `SEED.md` and
  stages a board of real work for the team-finder feature — not junk, and not
  self-referential (it ranks *your* feature, not the template's own scaffolding).
- **A handful of genuine forks.** A few real decisions only the pair can make — the kind a
  designer and a dev actually want to talk through (e.g. how matches are scored, what the
  empty state looks like) — not busywork prompts.
- **At least one cleared slice.** `vend work` spends its budget down and clears ≥1 slice of
  the feature, leaving a real run-log record and a green build.

## Why this exists

A hackathon demo is a one-shot. The point of vend is that the *clearing* is repeatable: the
same seed, the same charter, a comparable board and a comparable slice — every time. This
target is how that consistency is checked. T-058-05 captures the first real drive into it;
later runs measure against it.
