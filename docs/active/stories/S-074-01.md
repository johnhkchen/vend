**Scope:** The executor boundary — src/executor/executor.ts (the `Executor` interface, today `id` + `dispense` only) plus its two impls (ClaudeExecutor in claude.ts, the openai-compat executor) — gains a near-zero-cost dispensability probe distinct from PATH presence. Its two consumers call it: the `vend doctor` preflight (src/doctor/doctor-probe.ts, a new Check) and the cast-time refusal path (src/engine/cast.ts:169–204 + the cast-core.ts classifier). Slices 1 and 2 of the epic. TIER_BUDGET, the shelf, and funding are untouched.

**Story acceptance:** On a machine where `claude` is on PATH but its config-store/Keychain is unreadable, `vend doctor` reports a FAILED `executor dispensable: claude` check carrying a fix-it hint (`claude login`; sandbox needs Keychain access) and spends no tokens; and a cast attempted in that same environment halts on the missing-capability amber andon with the named cause + hint (logged `outcome: missing-capability`, usage {}, cost 0) rather than a raw stack. When the executor IS dispensable, doctor passes the check and a cast is byte-identical to today (existing cast + claude tests stay green).

**Honest boundary:** The probe is a reachability/config-store readability check — near-zero-cost and FREE, NOT a live metered dispense. It is fixture-proven here via injected probe facts; proving it against a real denied-Keychain sandbox is the deferred live-verify (the 2026-07-13 field-report scenario), authorized at the counter, named here not hidden in a ticket. Does not touch TIER_BUDGET or funding (slice 3, sibling story S-074-02).
