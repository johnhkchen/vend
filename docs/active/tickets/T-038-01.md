## Context

**Give the per-cast wall-clock kill-switch headroom above the price — at the one seam that sets it.**
E-037's live sweep cleared 0 because `propose-epic` was guillotined at **72,785 ms** (its p90 time
envelope) on the board's heaviest signal, twice. The kill-switch is **`timeoutMsFor(budget)`**
(`src/budget/budget.ts:78`), which returns `budget.timeMs` verbatim and is applied at
`src/engine/cast.ts:216` (`timeoutMs: timeoutMsFor(budget)`; the `ExecutorTimeoutError → timed-out`
catch at :219). Because `timed-out` is a **censored** outcome (`recalibrate.ts:60`), a killed run is
excluded from the percentile sample — so the envelope **caps itself and prevents the data that would
raise it** (a censoring ratchet). Raising the tier percentile cannot fix it: the propose-epic
successes all sit in **66.9–72.8 s**, the censored runs at **~72–73 s** — the tail is censored out of
its own sample (`work/T-037-03/verdict.md`).

**The change — one factor, one seam:**

- **`timeoutMsFor(budget)` returns `budget.timeMs × HEADROOM`** — a warranted, documented constant.
  Pick a factor with real margin (the censored runs were within ~1% of the envelope, so even small
  slack clears *them*, but choose ~2× so the next heavier signal isn't immediately re-censored);
  justify it at the definition from the censored-margin data. Preserve the `assertPositiveInt`
  contract (coerce to a positive int).
- **Change ONLY the kill-switch.** `fitNext`/`canAfford` (affordability, `spend-core.ts`) and the
  shelf/`envelope` surfaces must keep reading the bare measured `budget.timeMs` (the price — IA-8,
  the meter must not lie). Confirm via codebase-memory that affordability does **not** route through
  `timeoutMsFor` (it reads the budget's `timeMs` directly), so this change is isolated to the runner.

**Document at the definition:** why p90-as-timeout self-censors (the ratchet), why raising the
percentile alone can't fix it (the tail is censored from its own sample), that the price stays the
honest p90 and only the runaway-guard loosens, and that **IA-14 (auto-widen the envelope on a high
censored rate)** is the deferred fuller rung this surgical fix sits beneath.

**Honest boundary:** this removes the guillotine deterministically; it does **not** prove the heavy
signal now *clears* — that live re-run is Frontier 1's next pull (now unblocked), not this ticket.
**P7 holds:** the macro wallet still hard-stops on total actuals (`spendDown` debits real time); only
the per-cast runaway-guard gains slack. **Open sub-question** (record the call in the work artifact):
affordability gates on the price (p90, recommended) or the headroomed timeout — recommend price,
since the wallet debits actuals and the macro total still bounds.

**Codebase-memory (use it — project coding-task suggestion):** confirm the timeout seam + that
affordability is isolated from it with the **codebase-memory-mcp** (project
`Volumes-ext1-swe-repos-vend`, indexed/ready) via `search_code`/`query_graph`, not raw grep:
- `search_code(pattern: "timeoutMsFor|fitNext|canAfford|timeMs", regex: true, mode: "full", path_filter: "^src/(budget|engine)/")` — the kill-switch seam vs the affordability reads, to confirm the change is isolated to the runner.
