## Intent — the bigger-picture play

E-046 shipped the typed-DAG substrate (`dag-core`/`graph-core`/`graph`) and proved it **deterministically
with stub nodes** — the right proof for the *machinery* (fan-out, join, halt, topological + parallel
execution). What a stub graph cannot show is the thing the substrate exists for: **real plays composing
into a graph and running concurrently.** This epic casts a real one — modest but genuine — through
`castGraph`, live.

**The graph (built from the 6 existing plays — no new play needed):**

```
survey (project → board path) ──┬─→ propose-epic (board → signal #1 → epic) ──┐
                                └─→ propose-epic (board → signal #2 → epic) ──┴─→ capture-note (both epics → a consolidating note)
              FAN-OUT (parallel)                                                JOIN (consumes both upstreams)
```

- **Source** — `survey` reads the project, produces a staged board path.
- **Fan-out** — two `propose-epic` nodes, each `adapt`ing the survey board's `produced` via the existing
  `parseBoardSignals` (`work-core.ts`) to take its signal #1 / #2 → a `ProposeEpicInputs`. **They run
  CONCURRENTLY** — `castGraph`'s wave dispatcher casts both at once. This is the load-bearing live
  proof: **two real `claude -p` casts in parallel**, which `runChain` (sequential) structurally cannot do.
- **Join** — a `capture-note` node whose `adapt` consumes **both** upstream epic paths (the multi-entry
  `NodeUpstreams` map) and writes a note consolidating the two minted epics. The first real **join
  consumer** — a node that genuinely reads two upstreams.

## The honest boundary — what this IS and is NOT (load-bearing)

- **LIVE, METERED spend** (real `claude -p` for survey + 2 proposes + note = ~4 casts), bounded by P7
  (recommend a budget that affords the 4 casts; the 2 proposes run concurrently). **NOT** a free proof.
- **This is the FIRST composed playbook, and it is deliberately MODEST.** The 6 current plays were not
  designed to compose richly (E-046 named "richer playbooks" as downstream) — so this composition is
  genuine but simple. Its value is proving the **substrate carries real plays + real concurrency live**,
  not delivering a profound new playbook.
- **It mints 2 real epics** (the 2 proposes) — real board mutation. E-043 (idempotent mint) prevents
  orphans; the settle **assesses their quality** (sound demand vs filler) and notes they may be
  un-decomposed (the E-046 partial-chain pattern). *(Alternative if board mutation is unwanted: a lighter
  `survey → [expand ×2] → note` variant that only STAGES signals — note the choice in the work artifact;
  the propose variant is recommended as the more meaningful pipeline.)*
- **The concurrency is the proof, not the artifacts.** Capture evidence that the two proposes actually
  ran **overlapping in time** (the wave dispatcher worked live) and that the join received **both**
  produceds — that is what a stub graph and the linear chain can't show.

## Value to the design

Turns "the DAG substrate is built (stub-proven)" into "the DAG runs real plays concurrently, live" —
the first end-to-end composed playbook, and the live confirmation that `castGraph`'s parallelism +
multi-upstream join are real, not just unit-true. It also exercises the macro plays (survey, propose,
note) in a genuinely new shape, surfacing any real-play wiring gaps the stub example couldn't.
