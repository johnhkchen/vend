## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Generalize E-001's **hardcoded** `DecomposeEpic` runner into an authoring + casting
layer. Today one play is welded into `src/play/decompose-epic.ts`; the engine turns
that into a **registry of authored plays** (BAML functions), each cast through the one
proven metered seam — render via `b.request`, dispense via `claude -p`, parse via
`b.parse`, gate, budget, log — with **no per-play hardcoding**. It is where the specs
we extracted by hand become *runnable*: `DecomposeEpic` (R1–R12), `ProposeEpic`
(PE-1–PE-7), and one-shot **sorceries** all cast through the same engine.

It is the **ramp play** (Green): it costs the most to build and makes every future play
cheaper to cast — the bridge from "the hardcoded play" to "any authored play."

## Value to the design

The v1 leap that turns the whole hand-cleared pipeline into a machine-cleared one. It
advances **author-once-run-forever** (**P1**) — a play is specified once and cast by
gesture — and **executor-agnostic** (**P6**) — via BAML's client registry, the same
authored play can later target open models. And it resolves the **E-006 friction
(F4)**: a one-shot planning *sorcery* casts through the dispense engine, not forced
through lisa's RDSPI (which is built for code *permanents*).

## Done looks like

- A play is **authored once** as a BAML function (sorcery or permanent) and registered —
  no new TypeScript runner per play.
- `vend <n>` **casts any registered play** through the single metered seam — paying its
  warranted budget (mana), resolving against its gates, logged countably.
- **At least two plays** cast through the one engine (e.g. `DecomposeEpic` + a sorcery,
  or `ProposeEpic`) — proving *author once, cast forever*, not a second one-off.
- A one-shot planning sorcery runs as a **cast, not a lisa RDSPI ticket** (F4 resolved).
