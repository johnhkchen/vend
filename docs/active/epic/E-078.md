_Proposed by Vend's `propose-epic` play._

## Intent — the bigger-picture play

A live cold-start field report surfaced two trust wounds at the counter. First, `vend chain --help` parses `--help` as the signal argument and launches a real metered cast — field-confirmed as a ~15k gate-refused burn for the act of asking for help, reproduced at HEAD. The fix is one parse-level guard: `--help`/`-h` anywhere in any vend invocation prints usage and exits before any dispatch, making a help gesture structurally incapable of spending. Second, the P1..P7 labeled-invariant charter convention is tribal knowledge: the epic schema requires `advances` codes that resolve against the charter, but a fresh organic charter ships no labels, and the operator discovers the convention only by autopsy — cites dangle, `advances` collapses to [], and the value gate refuses with 'advances nothing', which teaches even less. The play is to make the convention teach itself at all three surfaces where a newcomer collides with it: the value/bounds refusal names the actual cause when the charter has no labeled invariants ('your charter has no labeled invariants (P1..) — label them or cite none'), `vend doctor` gains a charter-convention check (green with the count of labeled invariants; amber with the how-to when none), and `vend init`'s charter template ships the labels with a one-line comment saying casts cite them.

## Value to the design

Closes the gap between the two-gesture promise and the zeroth gesture: finding out how to use vend becomes free by construction, not by convention (P2), and a help invocation can no longer spend tokens it was never allocated (P7). The charter-convention surfaces turn a stack-trace-grade failure into a refusal that names the cause and the fix (P3 — gates teach, not just verdict), so a fresh operator meets the P-label convention at init, at doctor, and at the first refusal, instead of reverse-engineering it from degraded cites. Both fixes convert field-reported trust damage into structural guarantees, which is the clearing house's core promise made true at the counter.

## Done looks like

The field report's repro is dead: `vend <any-command> --help` (and `-h`) at HEAD prints usage and exits with zero tokens metered — no cast is launched, no ledger line is written. On a project whose charter has no labeled invariants, the value/bounds refusal names the missing-labels cause and states the labeling fix; `vend doctor` shows the charter-convention check (green with a count when labels exist, amber with the how-to when none); and a fresh `vend init` charter template carries P-labeled invariants plus the one-line comment that casts cite them. A cold-start operator can go from install to first legible refusal without ever paying for help or meeting the convention as a mystery.

## Context & constraints

Grounded in the live cold-start field report staged on the demand board (commit fa41c20), with the help-burn reproduced at HEAD and killed mid-cast. Prerequisites: none — `vend init` and `vend doctor` are shipped surfaces (E-061 onboarding arc), and the value gate already refuses on empty `advances`; this epic changes what those surfaces say and adds one parse-level guard, not their verdict logic or the charter schema (advances codes still must resolve — only the convention's legibility changes). Non-goals respected: nothing here converses (N1), supervises (N2), or executes (N4). Note on the tier: the signal cited P5, but local-first is untouched — this card cites P2/P3/P7, which the signal's own rationale actually describes. Right-sized per PE-7: the guard is one check plus a regression test, and the convention work is three small message/template/check surfaces — together one ~2-hour block.
