## Intent — the bigger-picture play

`decompose-epic` currently materializes every story as a ten-line shell whose entire body is "Materialized by Vend's `decompose-epic` play — N ticket(s)." This is not repo drift: the identical shell reproduces in every vend-native repo (vend's own board in the machine-decomposed era, boilerplate-demo from birth), because it is the play's authored output degraded to the minimum its gates accept — the play gates ticket structure, but nothing checks a story body, and an ungated output of a probabilistic process converges to whatever the gates allow. The story layer is supposed to be the middle contract between epic intent and ticket execution: scope, acceptance, honest boundary, ticket-DAG rationale, out-of-slice — the shape the hand-authored S-035-01 still demonstrates. Fix it at the playbook so it fixes everywhere: extend the decompose schema and render so stories carry that contract, add an enforceable story-completeness gate, and freeze the bar as a gold-master. This matters NOW because the routing arc (Two-Seat Operator: overflow, cross-vendor `agent:` routing, cross-review) needs the story as its allocation unit — boilerplate-demo already shows routing applied by hand, ticket-by-ticket, because the machine emits the unit blank — and because cross-vendor workers arrive coldest (codex, `AGENTS.md`-only, no accumulated context) and today receive a two-sentence ticket with the intent two hops away.

## Value to the design

Applies vend's own doctrine to its flagship play: gates are the contract (P3) — decomposition output becomes trustworthy without a human re-reading each story, which is the only honest meaning of "clears intent into allocatable work." Pays the specification once at the playbook (P1): one authored fix amortizes across every future decompose cast in every vend-native repo, exactly the way the defect did. And it makes the allocatable unit genuinely portable — a story an agent picks up cold is what executor-agnostic routing (P6) routes, overflows, and cross-reviews; the story contract is the "portable brief" the persona work names as the durable skill vend industrializes.

## Done looks like

Casting `decompose-epic` against a fixture epic materializes stories whose bodies carry the contract — scope, acceptance beyond a single checkbox, honest boundary, ticket DAG with wave rationale, out-of-slice — with no shell bodies on the board. Replaying today's ten-line shell against the new gate is refused with a named andon (story-incomplete), not silently accepted. The passing run is committed as a gold-master EXPECTED-OUTCOME so the bar is re-runnable, and `rdspi-workflow.md` documents the story layer's job and its readers (executing agents — especially cross-vendor cold ones — and downstream allocation policy), so the contract reaches every worker's context through lisa's injection.
