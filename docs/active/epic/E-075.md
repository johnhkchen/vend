## Intent — the bigger-picture play

One vocabulary-and-honesty pass across vend's read surfaces so that what a visitor reads is legible and never over- or understates reality. The persona research named jargon-heavy SVG faces the #1 weak link ('if faces stay jargon-heavy the SVG is just a reframed text wall'), and the 2026-07-12 UX ride-alongs (docs/active/pm/ux-rubric-survey.md, dims 6/7/9) found the audit/ledger lines and shelf labels speak in tokens ('E1 walk-away', 'intervention bit unrecorded', 'censored', 'andon rate', '(default — no runs yet)' for plays that actually have 1–2 real runs, '(no actions)', 'wrote … 1 groups, 198 cards'). The play is to say each of these in words a person could speak aloud at a kitchen table, keeping the honest content exactly intact — not softening it. Four confirmed edges: (1) strip residual technical tokens (Baml…/Ci…/Claude p…) from SVG card faces (src/present/translate.ts); (2) the same plain pass on the audit and ledger lines an operator reads; (3) shelf-honesty wording — replace the collapsing '(default — no runs yet)' with '(default — N runs, measured at 3)' so a play with real-but-thin runs isn't mislabeled 'none', and drop the cryptic '(no actions)' header leak from the bare-vend shelf (src/shelf/shelf-row.ts, home-shell.ts); (4) fix the 'wrote … 1 groups, 198 cards' grammar AND probe the grouping degeneracy it exposed — one group for a 198-card board suggests grouping collapses on a mostly-done board. This is the first half of the strip-then-probe fork; the render-and-watch designer probe follows on the improved surface, not here.

## Value to the design

Directly advances P5: the visual and trust surfaces ARE the non-dev channel, and jargon-heavy faces collapse their entire value — a legible face is the difference between a shelf a non-dev can read and a wall they can't. Advances P3 by extending the gate contract to copy: 'labels that never lie' means a shelf row that says 'measured at 3' when it has 1–2 runs, not 'none'; honesty is enforced in the words, not just the numbers. Strengthens the core clearing feature by making the surface an operator reaches for actually self-describe what it's for and whether it's trustworthy.

## Done looks like

A person with no vend vocabulary can read an SVG card face, an audit line, a ledger line, and a shelf row and correctly say aloud what each means — with zero residual Baml…/Ci…/Claude p… tokens leaking onto any face (the existing faceJargon predicate stays empty). A play with 1–2 real successful runs reads '(default — N runs, measured at 3)', never '(default — no runs yet)'; the bare-vend shelf shows no '(no actions)' header leak; the write line reads with correct grammar ('1 group'/'N groups'). The grouping degeneracy on a mostly-done board is either fixed (a full board splits into meaningful groups) or honestly documented as expected behavior with the reason. The honest content of every reworded line is unchanged — nothing is softened to read nicer.
