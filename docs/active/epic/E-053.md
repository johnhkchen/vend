## Intent — the bigger-picture play

The funding guard a cast runs under (`fundingEnvelope`, E-050) currently has no rational bounds, so it swings to both unhealthy extremes. **Too tight:** a well-calibrated play funds at its bare p90 (no headroom — correct per E-050), but p90 means ~10% of runs exceed it; `vend chain`'s propose just budget-exhausted at 176,101 against a 169,873 envelope — over by 3.6%, a pure tail draw, halting the whole chain for no good reason. **Too loose:** an under-calibrated/censored play self-funds at `maxCensoredActual × headroom`, which ran to ~733k on E-051's decompose — unbounded by anything but the observed tail. Neither is rational. This epic clamps the funding guard to a **band**: a **FLOOR (350,000 tokens)** — below it we don't care, every cast is funded at least this so a too-tight p90 never starves a real cast — and a **CEILING (700,000 tokens)** — the hard P7 wall, no cast is ever funded beyond it (runaway self-funding is rejected here). "We care when runs exceed 350k; we reject at 700k." The band is on the FUNDING guard only; the quoted price stays the honest p90 (IA-8), and the macro-wallet keeps authorizing on price and debiting actuals.

## Value to the design

Makes the budget hard contract rational in BOTH directions — the floor kills the guillotine-on-a-tail failure the dogfood just hit (a 3.6% overrun should not halt a chain), and the ceiling makes P7 a real bounded wall instead of an open-ended self-funding ratchet. It is the natural completion of the E-038 (time headroom) / E-050 (cold-start widening) calibration arc: those made the guard generous *enough*; this makes it generous *within sane bounds*. Directly advances P7 — the allocated envelope becomes a predictable band a human can reason about, not a number that swings from 170k to 733k run-to-run.

## Done looks like

`fundingEnvelope`'s token output is clamped to `[350_000, 700_000]`: a cast whose computed funding is below 350k is funded at 350k (so the just-`next` propose, p90 ~170k, would now run under a 350k guard and not exhaust on a tail draw); a cast whose computed funding exceeds 700k (E-051-style self-funding to 733k) is capped at 700k (the hard P7 ceiling). The QUOTED price / shelf / `formatEnvelopeLabel` is UNCHANGED (still the honest p90 — IA-8; the band is on the GUARD, not the price), and the macro-wallet still AUTHORIZES on price and DEBITS actuals (the band changes only what a cast runs under, not what it's quoted or charged). Proven deterministically with stub records (a below-floor case → floored; an above-ceiling case → capped; an in-band case → unchanged), no live model; the existing check gate green.

## Context & constraints

Grounded in current code: `src/ledger/recalibrate.ts` — `fundingEnvelope` (E-050) is where the clamp lives, beside `MEASUREMENT_HEADROOM`/`CENSORED_WIDEN_RATE`; it returns `{ envelope, widened }`, the per-cast guard. `src/play/work.ts:194-199` documents the seam this epic respects: "the wallet AUTHORIZES on `price` and DEBITS the actuals, while a cold-start/under-calibrated cast is FUNDED at `max(price, maxCensoredActual × headroom)`" — the band clamps that funding result, nothing else. `fundingEnvelope` is already threaded into `work.ts` (the macro loop) and `resolveStepBudgets`/`fundedStepDefault` (the chain, E-050/T-050-02), so the band flows through both paths automatically — no new wiring, just the clamp. Boundaries / out of scope: the band is on the TOKEN dimension (the policy's numbers are tokens); wall-clock keeps its E-038 headroom unchanged. Do NOT clamp the PRICE (IA-8 — the shelf stays the honest p90); do NOT change `canAfford`/`fitNext` authorization (it gates on price). The floor/ceiling are constants (350k/700k) — tunable, but pinned here as the rational default. If a play's honest p90 ever exceeds the ceiling (none does today), that is a separate "this play is too expensive to fund rationally" signal, not this epic's concern. Respects N4 (the allocation contract, not an executor). Right-sized (~one small feature block): a clamp in one pure function + an end-to-end confirmation.
