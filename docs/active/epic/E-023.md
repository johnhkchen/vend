## Intent — the bigger-picture play

E-022 settled the consistency contract (**IA-17**: gated validity, not lexical identity) and
ran the equivalence sweep that adjudicated each articulation play. Two closed as *by-design*
(expand 1.00, steer 1.00 — divergence is equivalent-diversity). **Survey was the exception:**
`genuine-disagreement` (0.00 over N=3) — repeated casts on the same project produce genuinely
different boards. And survey is **load-bearing**: it drives IA-1's single recommended pull, so an
unstable board erodes the product's spine more than any other play.

But E-022's judge compared **whole boards**, and a board can disagree in the *tail* while agreeing
on the *head*. The contract's real concern is narrower and sharper: **does the #1 recommended pull
flip run-to-run, or do the casts agree on the top signal and only re-order the rest?** The one
surviving sweep board put the obvious keystone first ("scaffold the empty `src/**` against 62
tickets") — a pick any sane survey makes every time — which is a hint, not a measurement. So this
epic **measures the head**, then converges **only if the head is actually unstable** — you
converge the load, not the noise.

## Value to the design

The **first enforcement** of the IA-17 contract, not just its statement (P3 — gates are the
contract). It either (a) converges survey's recommendation so the home's #1 pull is stable
run-to-run — the consistency the vision promises, made true for the one place it most matters — or
(b) measures that the head is already stable and amends the contract to say *survey's tail
divergence is by-design*, saving a lever nobody needed. Measure-then-decide (E-014/E-022): don't
build convergence before knowing the head moves.

## Done looks like

- **The measurement:** a **top-pick stability probe** — extend the equivalence harness to isolate,
  per cast, survey's **#1 (and top-k) ranked signal**, and judge whether the *head* is stable
  across N casts independently of the tail. The head-vs-tail verdict the contract needs.
- **The decision, enforced:**
  - **head stable → accept.** Amend IA-17: survey's divergence is *tail re-ordering, by-design*;
    no lever; the contract is *more* precise (the #1 pull is consistent; the ranking below it
    isn't promised). Close.
  - **head flips → converge (bounded build).** A **consensus-cast convergence**: cast survey N×,
    use the existing equivalence judge as a **gate** to converge the N boards to their agreed
    top-ranked signal, stage the consensus board. The judge becomes a gate, not just a meter.
- A clear contract amendment either way (`IA-17` / `information-architecture.md`), evidenced.

- **First slice = the measurement + the warranted decision** (~1 block): the top-pick stability
