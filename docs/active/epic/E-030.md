## Intent — the bigger-picture play

IA-2 splits the home into **Board** (demand — ranked signals; the current `vend` menu) and **Shelf**
(supply — the authored playbooks). The supply half isn't surfaced yet: there is no view that lists
the registered plays with **what each is for** and **what it warrants spending**. This epic builds it
— and the "warranted budget" is the point: not the static `budgetForTier` guess the board menu shows,
but each play's **recalibrated envelope from its own run-log actuals** (E-013/IA-12 — the measured
fat tail, with a confidence read: how many runs fed it, cold-start to the authored default when a
play has no history). It is the same lesson E-025 taught the spend loop — *use the measured price,
not the static default* — applied to the **display** the human reads before allocating: the Confirm
default (IA-6) made legible and **earned**, so "see what it costs before you pay it" is literally true.

## Value to the design

Closes the supply half of Home (IA-2) and makes P7's "budget calibrated from data, not guessed" a
*visible* surface, not just an internal recalibration. Advances **P1** (author-once — the authored
playbooks are the durable supply, shown with their worth) and **P7** (the warranted budget is
measured, with its confidence honestly shown — a thin-sample envelope says so). Rendered in the
clean-typographic key (E-029 DL-6/DL-9): flat rows, worth · envelope · confidence, **no card chrome**.

## Done looks like

- **Plays carry their worth:** a short role/summary on the Play contract (what each playbook is *for*),
  set on the six registered plays — the supply's "worth" column.
- **The shelf shows warranted budgets:** each playbook paired with its **recalibrated envelope** from
  the run log (E-013), with a **confidence** read (measured over N runs · or *default* when a play has
  no history yet, honestly labelled — never a guess dressed as measured, the E-026 lesson).
- **A `vend shelf` surface:** lists the authored playbooks (supply) — worth · warranted budget ·
  confidence — in the clean-typographic key (DL-6/DL-9: flat, no boxed cards).
- **Proven free + deterministic:** `vend shelf` over the live run log shows the six plays with their
  measured envelopes (propose/decompose measured over their many runs; survey/steer/expand thinner;
  note cold-start) — no LLM cast.

## Context & constraints

- **Cites:** `src/engine/play.ts` (the `Play` contract + `registry` — add the `summary`/worth field;
  enumerate the supply); `src/shelf/menu.ts` + `gather.ts` (the existing render + `formatBudget` +
  `budgetForTier` — the board menu to mirror, and the *static* default this replaces with the
  recalibrated one); `src/ledger/recalibrate.ts` (E-013 — the per-play warranted envelope + its
  `confidence`); `src/log/run-log.ts` (`readRuns`/`forPlay` — the actuals source); `design-language.md`
  DL-6 (the shelf beneath the board) + DL-9 (no card chrome) + DL-3 (type hierarchy); IA-2 (shelf =
  supply). The six registered plays are the supply (decompose/expand/note/propose/steer/survey).
- **Honest confidence (the E-026 lesson):** a recalibrated envelope from a thin sample must *say*
  it's thin (cold-start to the authored default when N=0 — labelled *default*, not measured); never a
  guess dressed as measured. Reuse `recalibrate`'s `confidence` (sample size + censored count).
- **Non-goals:** changing what `vend` (the board) shows — this is the *supply* view beside it, not a
  board change; a full Home composite that fuses board+shelf (DL-6 names it; this builds the shelf
  half, the fusion is downstream); per-play *value tiers* (a tier is a signal/demand concept, not a
  tool's — a play's worth is its role, its envelope is its budget); editing recalibration math (E-013
  owns it). Not an executor (**N4**).
- **First slice = the worth field + the warranted-budget shelf + `vend shelf`** (clean-typographic).
  **Out:** the board+shelf Home fusion, value tiers on plays.
