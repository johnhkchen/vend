## Intent — the bigger-picture play

The E1 trust gate the macro-wallet rests on has two kinds of evidence that must never be conflated:
**attested** back-fill (post-hoc, `attest-intervention.ts` writes an `intervenedAttestation` marker —
"didn't-break") and **forward** live self-reports (`vend run`/`vend work --intervened`, the real
measurement). E-026's verdict counted the combined ledger — 13 attested + 2 forward — as "15 genuine
forward carriers, 93%" and declared the go *forward-confirmed*, when the forward-only sample was 2.

The root cause is mechanical and below the verdict: **`reviveRecord` (the run-log reader) strips the
`intervenedAttestation` marker** — the raw JSONL carries it, but every record `vend audit` sees has
it removed, so the rate is computed over an undifferentiated pile. This epic surfaces the marker on
read and **splits the walk-away stat into `attested` and `forward`**, so the audit shows both and a
verdict cites the forward-only count. It is the structural guard that makes the E-026 over-claim
*impossible to repeat*, not just corrected after the fact.

## Value to the design

Protects the integrity of the single most load-bearing number in the product's case (P3 — the trust
contract must be honestly quantified; an audit that reads attested-as-measured is a fabricated
contract). It is the read-side complement of the honesty discipline the back-fill instrument already
honored on the write side (it *marked* the attestation; the reader just wasn't using the mark). Makes
the "2/10 genuine forward, accruing" state legible at a glance, so the go's provisionality is visible
in the tool, not just the prose.

## Done looks like

- **The marker survives read:** `reviveRecord` surfaces a normalized provenance flag (an
  `intervenedAttestation` present on the line ⇒ `attested: true`), so existing back-fill records are
  correctly classified **without rewriting them**; live records read as forward.
- **The stat splits:** `auditWalkAway`'s `InterventionStat` gains `attested` and `forward`
  sub-readings (reported / intervened / rate each), split on the flag; the combined `reported`/`rate`
  stays for back-compat.
- **The render shows both:** `vend audit` prints the combined walk-away rate **and** the split —
  "forward (live): X/Y · attested back-fill: 13/13" — so the forward count is the one a verdict cites.
- **Proven free + deterministic** on the live ledger: forward reads **2** (1 intervention), attested
  reads **13/13** — no cast needed.
