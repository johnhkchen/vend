## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Now that a run's work is stamped with its seat of execution (E-071), route the completed diff of one seat's work to the OTHER configured executor seat and make that seat's adversarial verdict a gate the work must pass before it clears. This is the cross-review rung of the Two-Seat Operator's practice ladder — the point where vend stops merely scheduling two lanes and starts making them check each other. Its value is differentiation the persona can feel: a diff written on the claude lane is refuted-or-passed by the codex lane (and vice-versa) with no human in the loop, so 'two subscriptions' becomes 'two-party review' — a guarantee a lone executor structurally cannot make about its own output. The play matters NOW because it only became expressible once seat routing existed; it is the highest-differentiation rung and the natural successor to overflow routing.

## Value to the design

Realizes cross-vendor adversarial review as a first-class, enforceable gate — advancing P3 (the gate, not human trust, is the contract that a diff cleared), P6 (the review is only possible because the executor boundary is real: it routes across two independent executors rather than assuming one), and P4 (the verdict is a deliberate autonomous gate event, not a live approval step). It deepens the core clearing feature: work does not settle into 'cleared' until a second, differently-biased executor has tried and failed to refute it, raising the amount of agent output one operator can trustably deploy — verification, not budget, being the real ceiling.

## Done looks like

Running an applicable playbook with two executor seats configured causes the completed diff produced on seat A to be routed to seat B, which emits an adversarial pass/fail verdict that is recorded on the run ledger as a gate outcome. A diff seat B refuses does NOT clear; a diff it passes clears with its cross-review verdict attached — and the whole exchange happens without human approval in the loop. Observable at the epic level: a ledger line carrying a cross-vendor verdict, and a demonstrable refusal path where an intentionally-bad diff is blocked by the other seat.

## Context & constraints

Grounds against the seat-routing substrate cleared in E-069/E-070/E-071: RunRecord now carries `seatOfExecution` (S-071-01) and cast stamps it — that field is this gate's routing key and is the load-bearing prerequisite. PREREQUISITE (name, don't absorb): a real, invokable SECOND executor seat. Today vend only ever casts on the claude lane (per E-071 go-and-see); codex-lane capture runs through lisa. A contracted way to hand a diff to the other seat FOR REVIEW (not execution) may need wiring first — if it does not yet exist, that adapter is its own prerequisite epic, not hidden scope here. NON-GOALS respected: N4 — vend routes the diff and records the verdict; the reviewing SEAT (lisa/executor) performs the actual review, vend never reviews. N2 — this is an autonomous gate, not a human approval dashboard. N1/N3 — it operates on the reusable playbook's gated run, not a chat or one-off. OUT OF SCOPE: authoring per-playbook review rubrics (that is authoring-time spec under P1, paid once), adding new executors themselves, and the release-day gold-master bake-off (a separate named-playbook rung). PE-7 flag: routing-the-diff and enforcing-the-verdict-as-a-blocking-gate together may exceed one ~2-hour block; if DecomposeEpic finds it does, this is legitimately two epics (route-the-diff-for-review, then the-cross-review-clear-gate) — split rather than inflate.
