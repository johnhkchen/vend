## Intent — the bigger-picture play

E-037 ran the macro-wallet live and **cleared 0**: `propose-epic` was killed at **72,785 ms** — its
own recalibrated p90 time envelope — on the board's heaviest signal, twice (`work/T-037-02/
sweep-log.md`). The grounding shows why this is **structural, not a one-off thin number**:

- The runner derives its per-cast wall-clock kill from **`timeoutMsFor(budget)`** (`budget.ts:78`),
  which returns `budget.timeMs` verbatim. The cast seam applies it at **`cast.ts:216`**
  (`timeoutMs: timeoutMsFor(budget)`). So **the timeout IS the price** — the p90 time envelope.
- That envelope is **p90 of SUCCESSFUL runs** (`recalibrate`, IA-13). A cast on a signal heavier
  than the historical p90 is **guillotined at p90 → `timed-out`** → and `timed-out` is a
  **CENSORED** outcome (`recalibrate.ts:60`), *excluded* from the percentile sample. So a heavy
  signal can **never** become a success that would raise the p90. **The envelope caps itself and
  actively prevents the data that would lift it — a censoring ratchet.** (The exact pattern E-014
  hit on tokens; the decompose 50k→120k hand-raise was its band-aid.)

**The insight that corrects E-037's own suggested fix:** the verdict floated "widen via the
percentile." **That cannot work here.** The 5 propose-epic successes all sit in **66.9–72.8 s**; the
two censored runs died at **~72–73 s** — *just above* the sample's ceiling. p95 of that sample, by
nearest-rank, is still the max (72.8 s) — **you cannot percentile your way past a tail your own
sample censors out.** The fix must give the **kill-switch headroom above the price**, not raise the
price percentile.

So: change **`timeoutMsFor`** to return `budget.timeMs × HEADROOM` — the per-cast **runaway-guard**
gets slack, while **the price stays the measured p90** (affordability via `fitNext` reads
`budget.timeMs` directly; the shelf/meter still report the honest envelope — IA-8, the meter must not
lie). A heavier-than-typical signal now runs to ~72.8 s × headroom, **finishes**, and lands as a
**success that de-censors the sample**. One factor, one seam.

## The honest boundary — what this fixes and what it does NOT

- **It deterministically removes the guillotine; it does NOT by itself prove the heavy signal now
  *clears*.** That live confirmation (a re-run that mints + accrues a *cleared* forward record) is
  **Frontier 1's next cadence pull**, now unblocked — not this epic. This epic ships the lever + a
  deterministic proof that the timeout no longer kills at p90.
- **Headroom is a warranted CONSTANT, not learned actuation.** Pick a factor justified by the data
  (the censored runs were within ~1% of the envelope, so even modest slack clears them; choose real
  margin — ~2× — so the next heavier signal isn't re-censored). The fuller form — **auto-widen the
  envelope when the censored rate is high (IA-14)** — is named in `recalibrate.ts` as a later rung
  and stays deferred; this is the surgical kill-switch fix beneath it.
- **P7 is preserved.** The macro wallet still hard-stops on **total actuals** (`spendDown` debits
  what each cast really took and stops when the balance can't afford the next price). Headroom only
  loosens the **per-cast** runaway-guard; it does not loosen the macro budget. A cast that runs into
  its headroom debits its real time — the wallet accounting is unchanged.
- **Open sub-question for the implementer:** should affordability gate on the **price** (p90,
  optimistic — recommended, since the wallet debits actuals and the macro total still bounds) or on
  the **headroomed timeout** (conservative)? Recommend price; flag it in the work artifact.
