## Forks

The genuine decisions only the human can make — each recommendation-first. Assent or override:

### Fork — Once E-059 lands the corrected positive gold master, do you measure the non-dev bet or extend the channel mechanics?
- **Why it matters:** The non-dev channel is hypothesis-grade — claim 2b (non-devs want a visual surface) is the PM desk's riskiest must-be-true, and it is unvalidated by any real user. Extending the mechanics commits another block-plus of spec effort to an unproven bet (overproduction risk if 2b is false); measuring first can confirm or kill the bet for a half-day; banking avoids sunk cost but leaves the loop half-built at 'board renders'. This decides how much more to invest before the bet is tested against a real human.
- **Options:**
  1. Measure-first — hand a real designer the rendered SVG board and observe where she gets lost (the render-and-watch probe), validating hypothesis 2b before building more.
  2. Extend the mechanics — pull the keystone (MCP graceful-degrade, #3) + the seed budget envelope (#2) so a full slice clears end-to-end on the seed.
  3. Bank E-059 and pivot off Set B — take the corrected gold master as sufficient proof and return capacity to another frontier.
- **Vend recommends:** Measure-first. The charter's go-and-see grounding and the PM desk both say validate the riskiest must-be-true before extending — a half-day render-and-watch probe should gate the next block of build, so you don't deepen an unproven channel blind.

### Fork — When `codebase-memory-mcp` is absent on a fresh seed (the keystone signal #3), how should decompose behave?
- **Why it matters:** This is a grounding-quality vs onboarding-friction trade-off and it shapes the entire design of the keystone signal. Degrade risks ungrounded decomposition (regresses charter P2 'grounded'); shipping the MCP raises friction against the two-gesture / local-first non-dev promise (P2/P5); hard-gate is honest but blocks the seed's full loop until the user acts. Only the human can set where Vend sits on that line.
- **Options:**
  1. Graceful-degrade — decompose proceeds without the MCP, logging an honest reduced-grounding note.
  2. Ship-the-MCP in the seed/init scaffold so decompose always has it (raises the onboarding surface).
  3. Hard-gate — decompose refuses with a clear 'install the MCP' andon: honest failure, no silent ungrounded clear.
- **Vend recommends:** Graceful-degrade with a logged honest note. The make-or-break (steer → board) never needs the MCP, so preserve the two-gesture onboarding promise and surface the reduced grounding plainly on the decompose path rather than silently lowering quality or hard-blocking the loop.
